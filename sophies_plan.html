The Science
What's Actually Going On in Your Body

You're pre-med, so you'll appreciate that there's a real mechanism behind every symptom — and a clean explanation for why these specific fixes work.

😴
The Energy Crash Cycle
High-glycemic foods (pancake bites, fries, white rice noodles, hot dogs) spike blood glucose fast, trigger a large insulin response, then crash blood sugar below baseline — that 2pm wall where you need a nap or a Red Bull.
Reactive hypoglycemia → cortisol spike → adenosine buildup → caffeine dependency to override it. The caffeine works short-term but accelerates the next crash. Fiber slows glucose absorption and flattens the spike entirely.
🚽
The Constipation Problem
Your diary shows roughly 3–5g of fiber per day. Women need 25g. Fiber feeds your gut microbiome and creates the bulk that activates peristalsis. Without it, colonic transit slows and the mucosa reabsorbs water from stool.
Soluble fiber (chia, oats) forms a gel via water absorption → softens stool. Insoluble fiber (broccoli, raspberries) adds bulk → stimulates peristalsis. Both needed. Chia + adequate water will produce noticeable change within 3–5 days.
💧
Dehydration is Doing Most of This
Water appeared 3 times in your entire week of diary entries. You're eating high-sodium processed food and drinking 3+ coffees daily (caffeine inhibits ADH → forces renal water excretion). Even 1–2% body water deficit causes measurable fatigue, brain fog, and constipation.
Dehydration reduces plasma volume → heart must increase cardiac output at the same workload → perceived exertion is higher → workouts feel harder. Your VO₂ is directly suppressed by this. Hydrating alone will improve your workout performance noticeably.
🦵
The Skater's Knee Pattern
Years of edge work creates specific imbalances: tight IT band (lateral hip and thigh), weak VMO (inner quad), and patellar maltracking. Any deep knee flexion — squats, lunges, stairs — compresses the lateral patellar facet against the trochlea.
Terminal knee extensions directly recruit the VMO via terminal extension against resistance — the only knee position where VMO fires maximally. Pilates rebuilds this alongside hip stabilizers. Expect real pain reduction in 2–3 weeks of consistent work.
💡 The Good News
Everything you're feeling has a clear, fixable cause. None of it is "just how you are." You were a competitive figure skater and cheerleader — your body's baseline is athletic performance. What you're experiencing is entirely nutritional and hydration-related. Fix those inputs, and the output changes fast.

Days 3–5: digestion improves. Week 2: energy stabilizes. Weeks 6–8: body composition changes visible. Month 3–4: full athletic form.

🦵 Daily 5-Min Knee Care (Do Every Day — This Actually Works)
Terminal Knee Extensions (TKEs) — band behind knee, straighten from 30° bend. 3×15. Directly targets VMO — most important exercise for your specific knee pattern.

Straight Leg Raises — lie flat, raise one leg 12 inches, hold 2 sec. 3×15. Quad strength with zero knee flexion = zero pain.

IT Band Foam Roll — 60 sec each side. Lateral patellar tracking from skating is driven by a tight IT band. Rolling reduces this significantly.

Calf Raises — 2×20. Reduces patellar tendon load by distributing force through the gastrocnemius.

Do these while coffee brews. 5 minutes. Real knee improvement within 2–3 weeks.
